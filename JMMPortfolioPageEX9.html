About Jay

I grew up in Indiana and moved to Music City in 1992.
I joined a band and got to take the ride of my life.
BR5-49 allowed me to travel the world. I got to live out a dream.
The events of 9/11/2001 caused me to reevaluate my situation and
I realized I wanted to start a family and stay home to enjoy it.
We now have 3 wonderful kids. Riley, 8, Harper, 6 and Drake, 4.

Latest Projects

I built all of the video content for
The Guitar: An American Love Story
at the the Tennessee State Museum.
Here is one of those pieces where Vince Gill
talks about acquiring his first "real" guitar.

Gruhn Guitars is THE source for
the Vintage Instrument World.
Here is a glimpse into their
repair department.

SESAC is a performing rights organization
with headquarters in Nashville and offices in
New York, Los Angeles, Atlanta, Miami and
London. I cover their Nashville Music Awards
and Christian Music Awards every year.

Earl Scruggs is credited with revolutionizing the banjo
as we know it. The Earl Scruggs Center in Shelby, NC
will be a state of the art museum to honor the
bluegrass icon. I worked with Craig Havighurst
and Robert Gordon to create the video content
that will be used when the center opens.

Past Projects

How Rhythm & Blues and Rock 'n' Roll Tore Down the Walls of Segeregation is a class that is currently being offered at Vanderbilt University. I built all of the video content for it. It was an amazing journey that was filled with highs and lows. It makes me at once both proud and ashamed of our country.

Barry Beckett was a keyboard player/producer that was a member of "The Swampers". He played on an incredible diverse range of songs. The beginning of Bob Seger's "Old Time Rock and Roll", that's Barry. In the middle of "I'll Take You There" by The Staple Singers, Mavis Staples namechecks Barry. Bob Dylan, Paul Simon, Hank, Jr., Kenny Chesney, Aretha Franklin, Bob Seger, is just the beginning of the list that considered Barry a friend as well as a "go to" guy in the studio.

My old friend Jason Ringenberg asked me to help his twin brother Farmer Jason with a DVD project. These spots continue to air on PBS and this one was nominated for an Emmy.

I have been lucky enough to work on an extremely diverse range of video projects in the last 10 years. Most, but not all are related to music. Take a look and hire me to do your next project. My kids will thank you.
